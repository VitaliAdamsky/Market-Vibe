What is Funding Rate?

Funding rate is the interest rate charged on perpetual contracts. It is calculated based on the difference in interest rates between the perpetual contract and the underlying spot market.

Funding rate is calculated as the difference between the interest rate on the perpetual contract and the interest rate on the spot market. The interest rate on the perpetual contract is determined by the exchange, while the interest rate on the spot market is determined by the prevailing market conditions.

Funding rate is typically expressed as an annualized percentage. A positive funding rate means that the perpetual contract is charging interest to the market makers, while a negative funding rate means that the market makers are charging interest to the perpetual contract.

Funding rate is an important metric for traders because it can affect the profitability of their trades.
